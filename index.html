How ε is defined
Following Gondek–Haensel–Zdunik (1997), the dimensionless eigenfunction is ξ(r) ≡ Δr / r, the relative radial Lagrangian displacement. The animation maps this to a finite, time-dependent displacement Δr(r, t) = ε · ξ(r) · r · cos(ωt), so the radius of a fluid shell originally at r becomes r · [1 + ε · ξ(r) · cos(ωt)]. The linearized perturbation equations are valid in the limit ε → 0; here ε is enlarged purely for visualization (the computed ω and ξ are unchanged). For ε ≪ 1 the motion is invisible; for ε ≳ 0.3 the linear approximation breaks down. Values ε ≈ 0.05–0.2 give clear visualization while remaining in the linear regime.
